# November 3, 2025
Over the weekend, the SNAP program ran out of funding. This is a big problem because millions of low-income families and vulnerable individuals, who often lack a financial safety net, rely on this aid to afford basic, healthy groceries each month. A sudden disruption in funding means people immediately face food insecurity, struggling to put meals on the table. Technology could help bridge this gap by rapidly connecting those affected with alternative food sources. Mobile apps or simple text-message services could provide real-time updates and an easily searchable, geo-located directory of local food banks, community pantries, and free meal programs. However, several challenges would limit the effectiveness of a tech-based solution. Many SNAP recipients belong to demographics with limited access to smartphones, reliable internet service, or the digital literacy required to navigate new platforms, creating a significant barrier to entry. If I were designing a tool, I would call it "Pantry Finder." It would be a simple, low-data mobile-web application that, using location services, instantly displays the closest emergency food aid locations, their operating hours, and current inventory levels reported by volunteers, focusing on accessibility and ease of use for anyone with basic internet access. 
# November 10, 2025
Technology has significantly changed holiday connection and celebration (Claim). Evidence of this includes families sharing gift openings via video chat platforms like FaceTime or Zoom, and distant relatives exchanging digital photo albums instead of traditional paper cards or in-person visits. While this connectivity can bridge geographical gaps and include everyone, I believe it makes the holidays subtly worse by prioritizing digital interaction over physical presence and potentially eroding genuine, in-the-moment connection. Developers of holiday tech, such as smart toys and assistants, should absolutely be responsible for ensuring their products respect privacy and family time. They must implement robust data protection and default "do not disturb" features because companies have an ethical obligation to protect users, especially children, and prevent technology from disrupting the core purpose of the holidays: focused, quality time with loved ones.
